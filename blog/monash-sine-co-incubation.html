We've joined the Monash–IIT Bombay co-incubation program

By the Jing team· 12 June 2026· 5 min read

AUSTRALIA × INDIA

Jing Herbal has been selected to take part in the co-incubation program run by Monash University and the Society for Innovation and Entrepreneurship (SINE) at IIT Bombay, accelerating start-up and academic spin-out collaboration across borders.

Designed to accelerate innovation between Australia and India, this initiative gives emerging health and advanced manufacturing ventures access to world-class research, facilities, and industry connections. For a Melbourne-based wellness brand, it creates opportunities normally available only to large, well-funded companies.

What the program actually gives us

Research infrastructure. Access to facilities and academic expertise to do the unglamorous but essential work behind every claim we make: testing, substantiation, quality verification.

A bridge into India's startup & commercial ecosystem. Direct lines to engage with leading experts in biochemistry, and the supply chains of one of the most important sourcing regions in the world.

A network across two countries. Mentoring, and connections that span Australia's research strength and India's startup energy.

Why India matters for what's in your cupboard

The consumption of traditional herbs has always crossed borders. Some of the most consumed botanicals the world now associates with wellness (turmeric, ashwagandha, bacopa) have their deepest roots in India, where the growing conditions, the plant varieties, and centuries of cultivation knowledge produce something you can't replicate elsewhere.

Lakadong turmeric, for instance, carries some of the highest natural curcumin levels found anywhere. This program puts us closer to finding best-in-class herbs at their source.

Why we said yes

We've been building Jing on a simple principle: every ingredient should earn its place, and we should be able to show our work. That principle only means something if the people checking it are the best in the field, which is exactly what this program puts within reach.

#2Monash · Pharmacy & Pharmacology, globally (QS 2026)
500+Startups through SINE's programs
US$3.5B+External capital raised by SINE ventures

Monash is home to one of the world's leading pharmacy and pharmaceutical science faculties, ranked #2 globally for Pharmacy and Pharmacology (QS World University Rankings by Subject 2026), and Australia and the Asia-Pacific's number one in the field for years running.

SINE brings the other half: one of India's most successful startup incubators, where more than 500 startups have come through its programs and collectively raised over USD $3.5 billion in external capital. It's a launchpad with a genuine track record of turning early ventures into real companies.

Sitting between the two, academic rigour on one side and proven startup firepower on the other, is why we chose to participate in this program.

What this means for you

Nothing changes overnight; good sourcing is patient work. But over the coming months, this is the engine behind the next chapter of Jing: a deeper, better-evidenced range, built on botanicals sourced from where they genuinely grow best, and backed by the kind of research that lets us tell you exactly why each one is there.

That's always been the promise. This just lets us keep it better.

— The Jing team

About

Monash University

Monash University is Australia's largest university and a member of the Group of Eight, ranked among the world's top 50 institutions. It is home to one of the world's leading pharmacy and pharmaceutical science faculties, consistently ranked in the global top group for Pharmacy and Pharmacology, and number one in Australia and the Asia-Pacific in the field.

About

SINE, IIT Bombay

The Society for Innovation and Entrepreneurship (SINE) is the technology business incubator at the Indian Institute of Technology Bombay, established in 2004 to foster entrepreneurship. SINE provides start-to-scale-up support to early-stage startups, typically over a three-year incubation period. More than 500 startups have benefited from its programs, collectively raising over USD 3.5 billion in external risk capital.
