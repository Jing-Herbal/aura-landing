The five-minute collagen smoothie

With @ngocgemm· 23 June 2026· 3 min read· 5 minutes, blender included

PROTEIN × BLUEBERRY × 枸杞

"Give me 5 min and I will make you a protein smoothie that will make your skin glow too." That's how Gem pitched this one on Instagram, and honestly, we can't beat it. Four ingredients, one blender, and every ingredient doing a job — because collagen isn't something you drink so much as something your body builds, and building it takes raw materials.

What you need

Makes one big glass (or two polite ones):

  • 1 cup protein powder — Gem's measure; scale to your usual serve
  • 1 tsp Jing Aura skin powder 四味本草
  • 1 handful of blueberries, fresh or frozen
  • 1 tbsp goji berries 枸杞, soaked in warm water first
  • Milk or water to blend, to your preferred thickness

Why each ingredient earns its place

Protein powder. Collagen is a protein — your body assembles it from amino acids, and it can't assemble what you don't supply. A protein-forward breakfast is the least glamorous, most load-bearing part of any "glow" routine.

Aura. One stick carries the four botanicals plus Vitamin C and Zinc at 100% NRV — and Vitamin C is the one with the authorised claim that matters here: it contributes to normal collagen formation for normal skin function. Unflavoured, so the smoothie tastes exactly like it did before. The full dossiers are here.

Blueberries. Colour is the clue — the deep violet comes from anthocyanins, and berries are among the most studied fruits for antioxidant intake. They also do the heavy lifting on flavour.

Goji berries. The one ingredient from both traditions at once: a classical skin-and-eyes tonic in the Chinese kitchen, and rich in zeaxanthin and carotenoids on paper. Soaking them in warm water first is the difference between chewy flecks and a smooth blend — don't skip it.

How to make it

Soak the goji berries in warm water while you gather everything else — five minutes is plenty. Blend the protein, Aura, blueberries and drained goji with your liquid of choice until smooth. Taste, thin if needed, drink while cold.

A note on honesty

The nutrient claims above (Vitamin C, Zinc) are the authorised ones on Aura's label. The botanicals — goji included — are described by tradition and by research areas, not by proven skin outcomes. Skin renews on a roughly 28-day cycle; no smoothie changes that timetable, however good it tastes.
