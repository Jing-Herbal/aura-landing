The science · 實證

We separate what’s proven from what’s promising — on every label.

Most supplement marketing blurs tradition, hope and science into one glossy claim. We refuse to. Here is exactly how much weight sits behind each statement we make.

The evidence ladder

Four rungs of proof

Every claim on this site is placed on one of these rungs — and we tell you which.

Rung 1 · Authorised

Regulator-approved nutrient claims

Stated as plain fact. The Vitamin C and Zinc claims on Aura are drawn verbatim from the EU Register of authorised health claims. These are the only efficacy claims we make.

Rung 2 · Clinical

Human trial evidence

Described with its limits — sample size, dose, quality. Where human data on a botanical exists, we cite it and note whether the dose matches ours.

Rung 3 · Mechanistic

Lab & pre-clinical research

Labelled as “studied for”, never “proven to”. Cell and animal studies are genuinely interesting and genuinely not proof of a benefit in you.

Rung 4 · Traditional

Documented traditional use

Presented as heritage, not medicine. Centuries of recorded use in Chinese materia medica is a reason to study an ingredient — it is not, on its own, a health claim.

Ingredient dossiers

Four botanicals, fully footnoted

For each, we give the tradition, the research area, and where that research currently sits on the ladder. Citations are illustrative of the literature and provided for transparency, not as claims of efficacy.

01 / 04

Tremella

銀耳 Tremella fuciformis

Tradition · Rung 4

Known as the “silver ear” or “snow fungus”, tremella was prized in imperial China as a beauty and longevity food, famously associated with the Tang dynasty consort Yang Guifei. Traditionally cooked into sweet soups to “moisten” the skin and lungs.

Research area · Rung 3

Tremella polysaccharides can hold a remarkable volume of water and are studied in vitro for skin hydration and moisture retention — sometimes compared to hyaluronic acid as a water-binding agent. Human skin data remains limited.

Polysaccharides from Tremella fuciformis exhibit high water-holding capacity and are investigated as natural moisturising agents.Illustrative of the polysaccharide literature · in-vitro / pre-clinical
02 / 04

Goji

枸杞 Lycium barbarum

Tradition · Rung 4

The wolfberry has one of the longest continuous records in the Chinese materia medica, traditionally taken to “nourish the blood” and benefit the eyes and complexion. Still eaten daily across East Asia.

Research area · Rung 2–3

Goji is rich in zeaxanthin, carotenoids and Lycium barbarum polysaccharides. Small human trials have examined antioxidant status and carotenoid levels; the wider literature is largely mechanistic. We class it as emerging, not settled.

Small human studies report increases in serum antioxidant markers following Lycium barbarum supplementation.Illustrative · limited human trial evidence
03 / 04

Astragalus

黃芪 Astragalus membranaceus

Tradition · Rung 4

A cornerstone “qi tonic” root in Chinese medicine, used for vitality and to support the body’s defences. One of the most frequently prescribed herbs in the classical canon.

Research area · Rung 3

Its saponins — astragalosides — and polysaccharides are actively researched in laboratory models for effects on fibroblasts, collagen synthesis and skin resilience. This work is pre-clinical; we present it as a research area, not a benefit.

Astragaloside compounds are studied in cell and animal models for effects on collagen synthesis and skin repair.Illustrative · pre-clinical / mechanistic
04 / 04

Schisandra

五味子 Schisandra chinensis

Tradition · Rung 4

The “five-flavour berry” — sweet, sour, salty, bitter and pungent at once — classified as an adaptogen and used to support endurance and, traditionally, a clear complexion.

Research area · Rung 3

Its lignans, particularly schisandrin B, are studied for antioxidant activity and effects on the skin barrier in laboratory settings. As with the others, this is mechanistic evidence and clearly labelled as such.

Schisandra lignans (e.g. schisandrin B) are investigated in vitro for antioxidant activity and barrier-related pathways.Illustrative · in-vitro / mechanistic
